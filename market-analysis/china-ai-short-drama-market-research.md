## 3. The 投流 (Paid Traffic / User Acquisition) Model

### How It Works

The short drama traffic model is the industry's defining economic mechanism:

1. **Hook:** First 1-3 episodes are distributed free as "bait content" (投流素材) on platforms like Douyin, Kuaishou, WeChat
2. **Convert:** At a plot cliffhanger, users are redirected to a mini-program or app to continue watching
3. **Monetize:** Users either pay per episode (IAP) or watch ads to unlock (IAA)
4. **Reinvest:** Revenue is reinvested into more traffic to acquire new viewers

### Economics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Traffic cost as % of total cost** | 50-80% of total revenue |
| **IAP per-episode price** | ~1.2 yuan average |
| **IAP monthly subscription** | ~18 yuan |
| **IAA daily revenue per user** | ~0.8 yuan |
| **Paid conversion rate (2024)** | 5-8% |
| **Paid conversion rate (2025)** | 3-5% (declining) |
| **AI manga drama ROI** | 1.1-1.8x on traffic investment |
| **Head drama gross margin** | 5-7% for traditional; 50%+ for comedy emoji manga dramas |

### Traffic Cost Trends

Traffic costs are rising while conversion rates decline. In 2024, a typical overseas short drama cost $120-150K to produce; by 2025, it rose to $150-180K, primarily driven by traffic inflation. Domestically, the traffic-heavy model means that even profitable dramas see thin margins after 投流 costs.

### Model Transition: IAP to IAA

The industry is pivoting hard from pay-per-episode (IAP) to ad-supported free (IAA):
- 2024 Jan: Free model = 11% of market
- 2024 Oct: Free model = 50%
- 2025: Free model = 55-66.3%

Red Fruit (红果) pioneered this shift, essentially "flipping the table" on the paid model.

### "端原生模式" (Native App Model)

A key 2025 development: traffic is purchased to drive users directly into the platform app (e.g., Red Fruit), where they stay and consume content ad-supported. This has replaced the WeChat mini-program funnel as the primary growth engine.

## 5. IP Development Pipeline

### IP Source Hierarchy

1. **Web novels (网文)** - Primary IP source
   - 阅文 (Yuewen/China Literature), 番茄小说 (Tomato), 晋江文学 are the "upstream mothers"
   - 山海星辰 partnered with 飞卢小说网 for exclusive adaptation rights to 600,000+ works
   - Tomato IP adaptations: revenue grew 8x in 2025 vs. all of 2024
   - Total micro-drama adaptation authorizations: 1,600+ (5x more than long-form drama)

2. **Long-form drama spin-offs** - Growing
   - 284 new long-drama adaptation authorizations in 2024, up 18% YoY
   - Example: *庆余年之帝王业* (spin-off of hit drama)

3. **Original IPs** - Emerging from short dramas themselves
   - Short dramas are becoming "IP incubators" due to low cost and fast iteration
   - Potential to spawn 10-20 head IPs per month

### IP Monetization Chain

```
Web Novel IP --> Short Drama Adaptation --> Successful Drama
    |                                          |
    v                                          v
Licensing Fee                          Series Development
(one-time)                             Merchandise (手办, 联名)
                                       Character Licensing
                                       Cross-media (长剧, 游戏)
                                       "微短剧+" Fusion
```

### IP Licensing Economics
- Head IP annual licensing revenue: >2 million yuan
- Merchandise/character licensing gross margin: 50-80%
- Cross-industry fusion formats: 微短剧+文旅, +非遗, +电商, +运动

### Key Trend: Manga Drama as IP Fast Lane
AI manga dramas enable rapid IP testing at minimal cost (10-30万 per 100-episode series), allowing creators to validate IP market fit before investing in higher-production versions.

## 6. Regulatory Environment

### AI Content Labeling Requirements

**《人工智能生成合成内容标识办法》 (AI Generated Content Labeling Measures)**
- Issuing bodies: 国家网信办 + 3 other departments
- **Effective: September 1, 2025**
- Requirements:
  - ALL AI-generated text, images, video must be clearly labeled
  - Both **explicit labels** (visible to users) and **implicit labels** (metadata/watermarks) required
  - Platforms must verify AI content labels before content goes live
  - Unlabeled or suspected-AI content must receive risk warnings

### Short Drama Licensing & Filing System

**分类分层审核制度 (Tiered Review System)**

| Category | Investment Threshold (2025) | Investment Threshold (2026) | Review Level |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|-------------|
| **重点微短剧** (Key) | >=100万 yuan | >=300万 yuan | National-level (广电总局) filing |
| **普通微短剧** (Standard) | 30-100万 yuan | 100-300万 yuan | Provincial-level filing |
| **其他微短剧** (Other) | <30万 yuan | <100万 yuan | Platform self-review |

**Key Rules (Feb 2025 onwards):**
- ALL short dramas must display either a 《网络剧片发行许可证》 or a filing/registration number
- Platforms, mini-programs, and traffic distributors must not promote unlicensed dramas
- Filing thresholds were raised in 2026, effectively making it easier for lower-budget AI dramas to avoid heavy regulation

### "AI魔改" Crackdown (2026 Jan)

- 广电总局 launched a nationwide one-month special rectification campaign
- Targets: AI-modified versions of the Four Great Classical Novels, historical/revolutionary content
- Specifically bans: content that distorts original spirit, blood/violence, vulgar adaptations
- Platforms required to clean up violating content and discipline offending accounts

### Implications for AI Drama Producers

1. **Mandatory AI labeling** is non-negotiable from Sep 2025
2. The **raised filing thresholds in 2026** are actually favorable for AI dramas (most cost <100万, falling into the lightest regulatory tier)
3. Content involving **historical figures, classical literature, revolutionary themes** is high-risk territory
4. More specific AI short drama regulations expected in **H1 2026**

### Production Toolchain

A typical AI short drama production pipeline:

```
1. Script/Storyboard --> LLM-assisted writing (ChatGPT/文心一言/通义千问)
2. Character Design --> Image generation (Midjourney/Stable Diffusion/可灵)
3. Scene Generation --> AI video model (可灵/即梦/Vidu)
4. Animation/Motion --> Motion control + lip sync
5. Audio --> AI voice synthesis + background music
6. Editing --> 剪映 (CapCut) / Premiere
7. Quality Control --> Human review and refinement
8. Distribution --> Platform upload with AI content labels
```

### Key Platform: 可梦AI (Kemeng AI)
An integrated platform that handles the entire pipeline: script parsing, character design, intelligent storyboarding, video generation - all in one system. Claims to compress 15-day traditional production cycles to 1-2 days.

### Model Update Cadence
Top AI video players maintain at least a **semi-annual base model update** cycle. 可灵 updated to version 2.0 and launched O1 multimodal model in Dec 2025.

- **《霍去病》:** 3,000 yuan, 3 people, 48 hours = 500M+ plays

The cost reduction is dramatic but comes with caveats:
- AI dramas still require skilled operators who understand both AI tools and storytelling

- The low entry barrier has led to massive oversupply, driving down per-drama revenue

## 11. Risks and Failure Modes

### Industry-Wide Risks

**1. Massive Oversupply, Low Hit Rate**
- 90% of short dramas do not make money
- 90% of non-head AI manga drama companies are losing money
- New drama registrations reached nearly 40,000 in 2025 (record high)
- 80,200 new manga drama companies registered in 2025 (+37.1% YoY), 245,000 total by Feb 2026

**2. Content Homogeneity & Audience Fatigue**
- "Sweet romance + CEO + revenge" formula dominates
- Lack of artistic innovation leads to audience burnout
- AI-generated content is increasingly recognizable and may face user rejection

**3. Traffic Cost Inflation**
- 投流 costs consistently rising: 249B (2023) -> 330B (2024) -> 400B+ (2025)
- Paid conversion rates dropping: 5-8% (2024) -> 3-5% (2025)
- Margins compressed as CAC rises and ARPU stagnates

**4. Platform Dependency Risk**
- Red Fruit dominance creates single-platform risk
- Red Fruit's Jan 2026 decision to cancel真人保底 caused industry upheaval
- Platform policy changes can instantly invalidate business models
- Red Fruit batch-paused真人 projects, forcing pivot to AI

**5. Regulatory Tightening**
- Mandatory AI content labeling (Sep 2025)
- "AI魔改" crackdown (Jan 2026)
- Expected: more specific AI short drama regulations in H1 2026
- Content involving historical/classical/revolutionary themes is high-risk
- IP infringement lawsuits increasing (multiple cases in 2024)

**6. Overseas Market Challenges**
- 80% of overseas short drama companies are losing money
- ReelShort: $270% revenue growth but turned loss-making
- 中文在线: Q1-Q3 2025 net loss 5.2B yuan (-176% YoY expansion)
- Rising production costs overseas ($12-15K -> $15-18K per drama)

**7. Quality-Quantity Tradeoff**
- AI dramatically lowers production barriers but also quality floors
- "Race to the bottom" dynamics as thousands flood the market with cheap AI content
- User willingness to pay erodes when AI quality is perceived as inferior

**8. Copyright & IP Risks**
- Unauthorized adaptation of novels and copying of drama plots
- AI training data copyright uncertainty
- Multiple infringement lawsuits filed in 2024

### Specific Failure Mode: The AI Manga Drama Trap
Despite the hype, most AI manga drama entrants are burning cash:
- Many spend months producing dozens of videos with minimal viewership
- Tool subscription costs (AI model APIs) can be significant at scale
- The gap between "can produce cheaply" and "can distribute profitably" is vast
- Only companies with strong distribution (投流) capabilities or platform relationships survive

## 12. Latest Policy: 广电总局 Regulations

### Timeline of Key Regulations

**February 2025: 分类分层审核制度**
- Formalized three-tier review system for short dramas
- All dramas must display license or filing numbers
- Platforms cannot promote unlicensed content

**September 2025: AI Content Labeling**
- 《人工智能生成合成内容标识办法》 takes effect
- Mandatory explicit + implicit AI content labels
- Platform verification responsibility

**December 2025: "AI魔改" Crackdown**
- 广电总局 announced special rectification campaign
- Effective January 1, 2026, running one month
- Targets AI-modified versions of classical works
- Bans distortion of original works, vulgarity, violence

**January 2026: Threshold Adjustment**
- 重点微短剧 threshold raised: 100万 -> 300万 yuan
- 普通微短剧 threshold raised: 30万 -> 100万 yuan
- **Effect:** Most AI dramas (costing <100万) now fall into the lightest regulatory tier (其他微短剧), requiring only platform self-review rather than government filing

### Regulatory Outlook

- **Favorable for AI dramas:** The raised investment thresholds mean most AI-produced dramas avoid heavy-touch government review
- **Content restrictions tightening:** Historical and classical adaptations face increased scrutiny
- **AI labeling is mandatory:** No exceptions; non-compliance risks content removal and account penalties
- **Expected H1 2026:** More specific regulations targeting AI-generated drama content specifically
- **Industry self-regulation:** Platforms (especially Red Fruit, iQiyi) are implementing their own content review standards that often exceed regulatory minimums

## Summary: Key Takeaways for Business Feasibility

### Market Opportunity
- The combined short drama + manga drama market is approaching **1 trillion yuan** and still growing rapidly
- AI dramatically reduces production costs (75-95% reduction) and timelines
- The shift from IAP to IAA creates new revenue models favorable to high-volume AI production

### Competitive Landscape
- **Red Fruit dominates** with 2B+ MAU; platform policy is the single biggest variable
- **Tool ecosystem maturing:** 可灵, 即梦, 可梦AI provide production-ready pipelines
- **90% of entrants are losing money** - distribution and 投流 capability is the key differentiator, not production

### Critical Success Factors
1. **Distribution/traffic capability** - Can you acquire users profitably? (投流 ROI >1.1x is minimum viable)
2. **IP pipeline** - Access to quality source material (web novels, original IP)
3. **Platform relationships** - Priority access to 红果, iQiyi incentive programs
4. **Production efficiency** - Ability to produce at scale with consistent quality using AI tools
5. **Regulatory compliance** - AI labeling, content review, filing as needed
6. **Category selection** - Avoid saturated "sweet romance/CEO" genre; target underserved categories for platform bonuses (up to 30% share uplift)

### Risk Assessment
- **High risk:** Entering purely as a production company without distribution advantage
- **Medium risk:** Entering with strong 投流 capability and platform relationships
- **Lower risk:** Entering as a tool/platform provider serving the ecosystem
- **Key unknown:** Regulatory direction for AI-specific short drama rules (expected H1 2026)
